FYI: Asking For A Reference Letter

When you should ask

Ask a Professor with whom you have worked closely for a reference letter. Ideally you should have done well in a few classes with the Professor, and you should have had contact with the Professor during office hours, for an extended project, and/or in consultation regarding your desired career path. If you are not sure whether you should ask a Professor for a reference letter, go ahead and ask your Professor their opinion about it. Sometimes a Professor might say no, but they can still give you a good idea about another Professor you should ask. Again, think about which Professors you worked closely with, who know you well, and who have seen some of your best academic work -- those are the Professors you should ask for reference letters.

Craft a good question.

  • Reference a particular page/paragraph/line of the book, or a point in class notes.
  • Explain what you understand so far.
  • Then explain what part you have a question on.
  • Show a homework problem you have half-completed and explain where you got stuck.

Questions to avoid

  • "I haven't read the book yet, can you teach me all of topic X in office hours?"
  • "I missed class and didn't get notes from my peer, can you teach me all of topic Y in office hours?"
