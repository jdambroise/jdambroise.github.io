    Why is this class hard?

    Your skills must be demonstrable
  • Simply attending/participating isn't enough to pass.
  • To pass you must demonstrate certain math skills.
  • Skimming or skipping parts of class can have a big negative impact.

    • You must be willing to grow
  • Learning will challenge you in new ways.
  • Your old study strategies may or may not work.
  • You will need to learn new study strategies to succeed.

    • Why learn math?

    Humans need to be able to understand computer output
  • Why does this work?
  • What is the process?
  • What logic would give this answer?

    • Reasons and rationale are equally important as final answers.
  • How can we explain the math steps?
  • How do we know calculators are correct?
  • How could we check calculator results on our own?
    • Use calculators only as a check, not as a substitute for learning.

    When will I use math?

    Direct uses in careers include...
  • science research
  • working in a science industry
  • other math fields like finance etc.
  • teaching
    • Indirect uses include cognitive skills such as ...
  • breaking things down into parts
  • analyzing information
  • problem solving
  • abstract reasoning
  • perseverence and precision

    • Don't blindly copy apps

    Does communication help math?

    Talking and discussing helps with understanding the why and how.
  • Your perspective may be different than other students.
  • You might recognize one part, but need to ask about another part.
  • You can learn from seeing how other students do math.


    • Get to know the Professor
    • Communicate often and frequently.
    • The Professor should know you and your needs.

    Time Commitment

    This class is like a part time job.


    Devote at least 8 hours / week outside of class time.
    Including class time you can easily spend 10-12 hours / week.

    Question: Would one hour every single day be enough?

    Homework: Due Dates are Not Start Dates

    Recommendation

  • One week before deadline: open and read homework
  • 5 days before deadline: complete easy problems and get help for others
  • 2-3 days befor deadline: complete over half of assignment and get help as needed

    • How can I succeed?

    Be okay with past failures
  • Remember that failure is part of success!
  • Reflect on past failures and ask yourself what barriers led to your failure.

    • Find new strategies for success
  • Success in the first few weeks is central to improving moving forward.
  • Osmosis or passively watching is not learning!
  • Research new study strategies that will work for you.
