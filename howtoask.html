How To Ask Substantial Math Questions

Be prepared

  • Read the book in advance of class.
  • Take good notes and study your notes.
  • Understand that learning is an imperfect process.
  • Be patient, persistent, and start early!

Be emotionally mature.

  • Get help when you need help.
  • Don't fear questions, welcome them!
  • Find some math friends and discuss class material.
  • Admit what you don't know and then find out how to fix it.
  • Understand the consequences of procrastination.

Craft a good question.

  • Reference a particular page/paragraph/line of the book, or a point in class notes.
  • Explain what you understand so far.
  • Then explain what part you have a question on.
  • Show a homework problem you have half-completed and explain where you got stuck.

Questions to avoid

  • "I haven't read the book yet, can you teach me all of topic X in office hours?"
  • "I missed class and didn't get notes from my peer, can you teach me all of topic Y in office hours?"
